Container House

Overview Design Features Sustainable Features

Solar Panels

Solar panels are a sustainable way to power a home as they rely on renewable energy from the sun. Solar panels can be used to generate electricity or to heat water, making them a versatile and environmentally friendly option for powering a tiny home. The solar panels are mounted on the roof of the shipping container and are angled to maximize the amount of sun they receive. The electricity generated by the solar panels is stored in batteries and is used to power the lights, appliances, and other devices in the home and overall, has a better outcome than the traditional method of generating electricity.


Rain water collection

Rain water collection is a sustainable way to use water in your home. Rain water is stored within a large rectangular reservoir, and is transported to the irrigation systems, toilet, and sink. This is sustainably friendly, and has a positive impact on the environment, whilst also reducing the water bills.



Home Automation

Home automation is a technology that allows you to control your home from anywhere, and is a great way to save money and energy. Garden irrigation systems, heating systems, and air conditioners are all controlled from your phone or on automated schedules, even when away from the home. In the long term, this minimises the use of electricity and waste of water, due to the precisely timed schedules.



Double Glazed Windows

Double glazed windows are a great addition to any tiny home for a number of reasons. They help to keep the home insulated, which can save on energy costs, and they also help to reduce noise pollution from outside. It helps to keep the inside of the home warm in the winter and cool in the summer, which can be a real bonus in a small space.
